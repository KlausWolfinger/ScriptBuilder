Detailed Description

Intercept And Treatment Definitions and Methods.

Intercepts are sequences of up to three treatments (for example, tones and/or announcements) OpenScape Voice and its media servers repeat a specified number of times. The administrator defines the properties of each treatment, such as a tone's duration or whether an announcement is "barge-in" or "non-barge-in." Once OpenScape Voice and its media servers have finished playing an intercept's treatments as configured in the intercept, OpenScape Voice returns the calling party line to idle, except in the case of calls from analog lines.

OpenScape Voice provides a series of system pre-defined intercepts that cannot be deleted. In addition user-defined intercepts can be provisioned. The administrator can assign treatments to and unassign treatments from intercepts, as well as editing the properties of these treatments and changing the order in which OpenScape Voice requests the treatments within the intercept.

The default Openscape Voice treatments can be provisioned using the unsp__BulkAssignTreatments() method.
